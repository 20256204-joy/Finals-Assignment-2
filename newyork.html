New York

History

New York was inhabited by Iroquois and Algonquin peoples, colonized by the Dutch, became a British colony, and later a key state in US history. NYC grew into a global hub for commerce, culture, and innovation.

Famous Places

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a universal symbol of freedom, gifted by France in 1886. It stands on Liberty Island in New York Harbor and is a major tourist attraction.

Times Square

Times Square is the heart of Manhattan, famous for its neon lights, Broadway theaters, and bustling crowds. It is a major center for entertainment and tourism.

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is a group of three waterfalls straddling the U.S.-Canada border. Known for its breathtaking views and hydroelectric power, it attracts millions of visitors annually.
