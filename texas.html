Texas

History

Texas was home to Native American tribes, colonized by Spain, became part of Mexico, then an independent Republic in 1836, and joined the United States in 1845.

Known for ranching, oil, and cowboy culture, Texas is now an economic and cultural powerhouse in the U.S.

Famous Places

The Alamo, San Antonio

The Alamo is a historic Spanish mission and fortress compound in San Antonio. It is famed for the 1836 battle during the Texas Revolution and is a symbol of Texas bravery and independence.

Space Center Houston

Space Center Houston is the official visitor center of NASA's Johnson Space Center. Visitors can see historic spacecraft, meet astronauts, and explore the history and future of space exploration.

Visit Space Center
Space Center Houston

Big Bend National Park

Big Bend National Park is located along the Rio Grande in West Texas. It is famous for its vast desert landscapes, canyons, mountains, and diverse wildlife. Hiking, camping, and stargazing are popular activities here.

Famous Foods

  • Texas BBQ – Brisket and smoked meats, a cultural staple of Texas cuisine.
  • Chili – Spicy stew with meat and beans, often cooked in large gatherings.
  • Tex-Mex Tacos – Fusion of Mexican flavors with Texas ingredients.

Famous Artists & Celebrities

Selena Quintanilla

Selena, known as the "Queen of Tejano music," was born in Lake Jackson, Texas. Her influence on music, fashion, and Latin culture remains legendary.

Willie Nelson

Willie Nelson is a Texas-born country musician known worldwide for his outlaw country style, songwriting, and activism.
